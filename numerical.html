6.336: Numerical Simulation

This is a graduate course I took in Fall 2016 as a sophomore. The class itself focused on a variety of techniques for mathematical modeling and simulation, from nodal analysis, GCR, and model order reduction. You can read more about the class here

For the final project my team and I used various mathematical techniques to simulate traffic flow at peak hours. Primarily, we used Node-Branch and a Newton method to explore the steady state soltuions of congestion as people go to work in the morning. We used a dynamic equation based and Gausian smoothing to get accruate time-step data for people going home from work.

We explored this congestion by income brackets, based on United States Census data, as well as comparing carpooling and no carpooling. Using the real Census data in our case study region, we were able to construct a fairly reasonable model. The network was built from the roads and geographical decision points obtained from OpenStreetMaps. We also designed a GUI to show our results.

I was privledged to work with amazing teammates on this project, Chong Shi Kai and DeJuan Anderson.
