Social Moods

Sometimes I’m hopping between Facebook and Instagram and I realize just how different the content and general feel of the networks are. There’s a ton of overlap in my friends and followers on both networks, but the experience just feels different. That got me thinking a lot about how users can be so different on different networks, even with so much overlap.

That’s why I’ve been working on different machine learning techniques to explore and assign moods to people’s social networks. I can look at posts and their content using image recognition and deep learning, along with natural language processing. I can also weigh different users that might influence your network more by how long you’ve been connected on that network, interactions you’ve had, and more factors. The exact process varies by each network, because Facebook and Instagram and Twitter all have different mechanics that dictate how we interact. These calculations result in the program knowing the overall mood of your network at the given time.

But a user doesn’t really want just a metric of the mood, so I designed a more tangible experience in the form of a lamp. Each module lights up the color of your network and the entire module charges your phone at the same time.
