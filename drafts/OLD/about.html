About

When I was 8, I decided I wanted to go to MIT to be an inventor. Now I'm here and my mom is happy I never dreamt of building a death ray.

I'm a first generation college student, studying Computer Science with a minor in Design.

I live and am Vice President of MacGregor House, research at the MIT Media Lab, and tour guide.

I am a researcher in the Changing Places group and think about how to realistically model complex systems and make them into tangible user interfaces, which may or may not involve Legos.

I spend a lot of time thinking about how different problems and complexities around us can be simulated, understood, and engaged with. My interests range from natural language processing to computer vision and numerical simulation, but it's all united in the idea that I love telling stories with the data around me through math, design, code, and electronics.

Overall, I love learning and making cool stuff. And playing with Legos.
