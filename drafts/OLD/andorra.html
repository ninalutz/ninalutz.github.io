Andorra

Working with the country of Andorra, my lab sought to understand how people move through Andorra, especially during events.

I worked with my supervisor Ira Winder on an agent based model that reflected how tourists moved through the capitol of Andorra based on CDR (call detail record) data.

My main responsibility was developing an algorithm to distribute the tourists at different amenities based on their rough location and language. This was a modified version of a voronoi algorithm.

This was a great introduction to research for me, as I was a freshman during the project, and resulted in a paper that was accepted to Bloomberg's Data for Good Exchange.

This was the video and write up for some of the work. And this is an official update on the general approach my lab uses for these models, and it also explains how we apply urban planning, math modeling, graph theory, and agent based simulation to this and other projects.

Note the different amenities all have different categories (restaurants, shopping, etc), languages, capacities, and prices associated with them. The goal of the voronoi algorithm is to assign amenities to their respective cell towers and assign segments of the population to the individual amenities.
