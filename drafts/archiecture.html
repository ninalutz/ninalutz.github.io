Hi Bye Stand

This was my first time doing woodworking and even getting into furniture. It was for my first design studio class at MIT, 4.031: Design Objects and Interactions. I designed an end stand for my dorm room, one that would actually fit comfortably in the strange space between my bed and futon.

First there were some sketches and mini models of the stand. The design was refined and interated on during this process.

I settled on experimenting with the form of characters by hiding a dual message of "HI-BYE" in the stand.

Then I built the stand out of simple 2x4 planks. All of the woodworking was done without power tools; all handsaws and hammered in. Here are some photos of the final product.
