Hi, I'm Nina

I want to use computers to make us think about other people.

I study the role of visual media in problematic information (online hate, misinformation, scams) and how it targets and denigrates identities, particularly marginalized ones. Basically, I dig through a lot of the internet's garbage to inspire ways that we can make the internet, and the people in it, better. When I'm not doing that, I create methods and tools for other researchers to answer their questions about visual media.

I am a second year PhD at the University of Washington and the Centered for an Informed Public, where I am advised by Dr. Kate Starbird and graciously supported by an NSF Fellowship.
