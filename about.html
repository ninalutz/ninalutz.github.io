Profile

When I was 8, I decided I wanted to go to MIT to be an inventor. Now I'm here and my mom is happy I never dreamt of building a death ray.

I'm a first generation college student, studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science with a minor in Design.

I live and social chair at MacGregor House, research at the MIT Media Lab, and tour guide.

I am a researcher in the Changing Places group and think about how to realistically model complex systems and make them into tangible user interfaces, which may or may not involve Legos.

I spend a lot of time thinking about how different problems around us can be simulated with math and code as complex systems. And how embedded hardware can help us engage with these systems. My interests range from natural language processing to computer vision and numerical simulation, but it's all united in the idea that I love telling stories with the data around me through math, design, code, and electronics.

Overall, I love learning and making cool stuff. And playing with Legos.
