Profile

When I was 8, I decided I wanted to go to MIT to be an inventor. Now I'm here and my mom is happy I never dreamt of building a death ray.

I'm a first generation college student, studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Science with a minor in Design.

I live in MacGregor House, take photos for The Tech, and tour guide.

Most of my time is spent in the MIT Media Lab. I am a researcher in the Changing Places group and think about how to realistically model complex systems and make them into tangible user interfaces, which may or may not involve Legos.

I think a lot about computer vision and how machines process the visual and digital world. From dorm robots to emotive social networking lamps, I'm fascinated with how digital systems see our world and how to enhance our experience with their observations.

Overall, I love learning and making cool stuff. And playing with Legos.
